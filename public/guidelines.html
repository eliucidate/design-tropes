Design Tropes: Community Guidelines

  • About
  • Contact
  • Community Guidlines
  • Submit

As a community, Design Tropes has very few strict rules. That being said, we do have some guidelines for keeping content relevant and appropriate:


1. Design Tropes exists to document tropes and trends across the lifestyle industries. However, our site isn’t strict about how that documentation takes place; feel free to write with whatever voice suits you or your post. And, most importantly, have fun with content generation and documentation while maintaining professionalism.


2. Please be polite, kind, and respectful to fellow editors of the site. Please strive to resolve posting and editing disagreements peacefully. The bashing of specific design work, its creators, and/or fellow individuals is tolerated. Please discuss work generously and un-aggressively, even you disagree with its goal, message, or aesthetic.


3. Page vandalism and editing wars are similarly not allowed. If you find you’re having a disagreement with a fellow editor, then feel free to offer both perspectives in the article under debate. In need be, please know that wiki administrators reserve the right to suspend accounts that explicitly attack fellow users or the site itself.


4. Before submitting a new trope, do a quick search to see if something similar already exists. If it does, then consider editing that trope page rather than creating a duplicate entry. Trope names should be short and unique, and trope definitions should be distinct and clear. These guidelines are part of Design Tropes best practices.


5. Wiki best practices also include describing the reasons for which you made changes to a specific page. Your fellow editors might not always understand why edits were made, so feel free to describe the rationale behind your changes. Please don’t delete wiki content without providing a reasonable explanation for doing so.


6. Users are encouraged to correct minor problems (i.e. spelling, grammar, formatting errors, etc.) that they encounter while surfing the site.


7. Design Tropes is targeted at all audiences, so please refrain from posting overly explicit sexual or violent content. Non-relevant explicit material will likely be removed by administrators.


8. Design Tropes encourages communication and feedback! If you’d like to see a feature rolled-out or a community guidelines policy added, changed, or updated, then please don’t hesitate to reach out to us!
